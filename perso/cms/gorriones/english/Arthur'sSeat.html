24hrs Cycling around Arthur's Seat, Edinburgh, Scotland for Los Gorriones

 
July 18th & 19th 2009

Drew Sharkey will be cycling round this hilly 3 miles circuit for 24 hours on July 18th/19th on behalf of Los Gorriones.  Drew will hopefully cover around 100 laps and 300 miles in 24hrs hoping that this challenging goal will get anough attention to help raise money for the children of Los Gorriones. 

Support Drew's challenge by sponsoring him

Arthur's Seat, Edinburgh
Arthur's Seat is an extinct volcano, which erupted around 340 million years ago. At that time, Scotland was a very different place, located close to the equator. The rocks of Holyrood Park give us some clues about what it was like in the past - there are sandstones formed in warm shallow seas, layers of volcanic ash created by many explosive eruptions of the ancient volcano, and lava flows representing quieter times. The volcano has been eroded so that now less than half of it remains, and this allows us to walk into the heart of the volcano and see rocks created underground.

Drew Sharkey

My name is Drew Sharkey, I am 35 years old and come from Edinburgh, Scotland. I have a background in cycling and running so thought I could put this to good use raising money for charity.

The reason I chose Los Gorriones is quite simple. I spent a month in Peru a few years ago and fell in love with the country and the people. I was able to see the poverty first hand and soon after my wife, Jan and I came up with a plan of how we could help. I came across the Los Gorriones website and after a few months of email contact with Gil, I realised that he and Chantal were pretty special. What they were doing was their life rather than something they were doing with their life. We were impressed by this and decided pretty quickly this was the place we wanted to help. We became Emersons sponsors shortly after and the rest is history.

 
We are now in a position where I can begin going to Los Gorriones and my first visit will be in October for 2 months. Although it is just me who is going, it is a joint effort as it would not be possible without Jans help and support.
