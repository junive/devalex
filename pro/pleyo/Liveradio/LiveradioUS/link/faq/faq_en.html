Q: what are the left and right catalogues in the lower area of the homepage?

A: the right catalogue is our general databases of radio stations and podcasts.

You will also find in this section the contents you have added by yourself and the tracks you have bookmarked.

The left catalogue is the catalogues of your personal selections (=devices).

By default, this is your “web catalogue”.

If you have registered your device, this is the exact “mirror" of what you have on your device.

Q: What my device’s MAC address is? Where can I find it?

A: A MAC address is a network connected device‘s unique ID. A MAC address is composed of 12 digits, presented as 6 pairs of 2 digits split with “:” (ex: 12:34:56:78:90:12).

You can find your Phoenix Wi-Fi radio’s MAC address either directly beneath the device, on the product label or through the Phoenix Wi-Fi radio’s interface in the Configuration/Serial Number menu.

Q: How to add content to your selection?

A: First, try to find the content you are looking for in the general catalogue (the left one) either by typing the name of the content in the search engine or by navigating through menus.

Once you have found it, you can add it to your selection by:

  • Clicking on the“+” button of the content, the content will directly appear in the “my radios” or “my podcast” folder of your selection, depending on its type.

  • Dragging and dropping it from the general catalogue to your selection’s destination folder.

If you didn’t find the desired content in our general catalogue, you have the opportunity to add it by yourself.

Go to my radios/podcasts section of the left area and click on the “Add a radio” or “Add a podcast” link.

Then, enter the following information:

  • Name

  • Country

  • Website

  • Streaming URL ( this is link that corresponds to the audio stream, if you have some difficulties to find it, see the following Q/A)

Test the streaming URL, and submit it.

The content will automatically appear in the “my radios”/”my podcasts” section of the left area, and also in the “my radios”/”my podcasts” folder of your selection.

Q: How to find a streaming URL?

A: If the content is a podcast:

You just need to find and copy the “.rss” or “.xml” link of the desired podcast (iTunes format is fully compatible).

If the content is a radio station:

Finding the streaming URL in this case can be more complicated ;-).

You are looking for a radio station streaming URL that might finishes as “.pls”, “.m3u” , “.rm”, “.rtsp” or “.asx”.

One of the best to find it, is to use a software called URLSnooper 2 (download it here (http://urlsnooper.en.softonic.com/)):

  • Set up URLSnopper 2

  • Launch the radio station streaming

  • Copy/ paste the streaming URL to our “Add a station” module.

Q: How to register more than one device thanks to a unique account?

A: the “devices” section of the “Configure” pop-in is designed for this purpose.

There, you will be able to register all your “Phoenix-compatible” devices with either automatic registration or by manually typing the MAC address.

Moreover, you can also customize all your devices’ selection for the homepage by choosing one or another device from the option list in the upper area of the right catalogue.

Q: Can I synchronize all my devices’ presets?

A: just go to the “contents/presets” section of the “Configure” pop-in.

There, you will have the opportunity to choose one of your devices as the source of the synchronization (master vs. slaves) and to launch the synchronization.

The section also enables you to desynchronize your devices.

Q: How can I configure my presets?

A: First, choose the content you want to put on a preset.

Then, drag it over the presets bar in the lower area of the right catalogue, and drop it over the chosen preset number.

The preset will automatically become this new content.

Q: Where can I recover my bookmarks?

A: On the right catalogue, click on the “Bookmarks” tab (the “heart” icon),

You will find back all the content you have bookmarked either on the player or on your devices.
